# Skill Dimensions

Podium scores every story submission across five dimensions. Each dimension is scored 0–100.

---

## 1. Structure

The backbone of any memorable story.

| Element | What the coach looks for |
|---|---|
| Hook | Does the opening create immediate interest or tension? |
| Protagonist | Is there a clear person with a want or need? |
| Conflict | Is there a problem, obstacle, or tension? |
| Turning point | Is there a moment of change or decision? |
| Resolution | Does the story land somewhere? |
| Takeaway | Is there a clear "so what" for the listener? |

**Professional framework:** STAR (Situation → Task → Action → Result)
**General framework:** And... But... Therefore spine

---

## 2. Language

The texture and vividness of your words.

| Element | What the coach looks for |
|---|---|
| Specificity | Concrete details vs. vague generalities |
| Sensory detail | Does the listener see, hear, or feel it? |
| Active voice | Are sentences driven by action? |
| Word choice | Vivid, precise, or unexpected words |
| Brevity | No unnecessary padding or hedging |

---

## 3. Pacing

The rhythm that keeps an audience engaged.

| Element | What the coach looks for |
|---|---|
| Sentence variety | Mix of short punchy and longer flowing sentences |
| Rhythm | Natural ebb and flow, not monotone structure |
| Filler words | Um, uh, like, you know, basically *(audio only)* |
| Speaking rate | Words per minute *(audio only)* |
| Strategic pauses | Silence used for emphasis *(audio only)* |

---

## 4. Emotion

The dimension that makes stories stick.

| Element | What the coach looks for |
|---|---|
| Stakes | Does the audience understand what's at risk? |
| Vulnerability | Does the teller expose something real? |
| Universal theme | Does it connect to something the audience recognizes? |
| Emotional arc | Does the story move through more than one emotional state? |

---

## 5. Delivery

Measured only from audio or video submissions (Phase 2+).

| Element | What the coach looks for |
|---|---|
| Vocal variety | Changes in tone, pitch, and energy |
| Confidence | Steadiness, authority, lack of excessive hedging |
| Presence | Engagement and intentionality |
| Physical delivery | Eye contact, gesture, posture *(video only)* |

---

## Scoring

Each dimension is scored 0–100. Scores are surfaced as:
- A radar chart showing your current profile
- Line charts showing each dimension over time
- Narrative feedback from the coach per session

### Score Bands

| Score | Label |
|---|---|
| 0–40 | Developing |
| 41–65 | Building |
| 66–80 | Proficient |
| 81–90 | Strong |
| 91–100 | Exceptional |

---

## Tracks

### Professional Track
Focuses on structured business storytelling frameworks:
- **STAR** — Situation, Task, Action, Result
- **Problem → Stakes → Solution → Proof** — for persuasive presentations
- **The "So what?" test** — every story point must earn its place

### General Narrative Track
Focuses on the craft of storytelling:
- **Pixar Story Spine** — Once upon a time... Every day... Until one day... Because of that... Until finally... Ever since then...
- **The unexpected detail** — the specific, surprising observation that makes a story feel real
- **Emotional vulnerability** — the willingness to share something that matters
